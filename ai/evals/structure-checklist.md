# Eval: Structure

Check substantial changes against these questions:

- Does the material belong in this unit or lesson?
- Is the unit doing a distinct job?
- Are doctrine, module, ADLC, and long-game measurement connected where needed?
- Are any concepts duplicated without purpose?
- Are any claims or exercises orphaned from the builder's path?
- Does each major structural move know which source carries it: founder, engineer, personal life, or a deliberate combination?
- If personal material appears, is it doing structural work rather than merely adding color?
- Does the lesson or unit end with a usable move?
- If this is a unit bundle, does root `unit.md` carry the unit-opening narrative rather than only an index or source note?
- If this is a lesson, are lesson prose, vocabulary, exercise, kit action, artifacts, and signals distinguished even if they have not yet been split into files?
- Does each major builder-facing unit follow the reader-orientation pattern where appropriate: Scene -> Tension -> Distinction -> Tool -> Return?
- Has the major builder-facing unit been tested through a narrative validation draft, or is there a clear reason that pass does not apply?
- Is the opening scene or situation returned to with changed understanding, rather than abandoned after the concept is explained?
