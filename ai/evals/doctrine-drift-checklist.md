# Eval: Doctrine Drift

Check substantial changes against these questions:

- Does the change preserve the Organic Intelligence versus Artificial Intelligence distinction?
- Does the human still own risk, judgment, origination, and review?
- Does the change preserve **steering** — the human's in-flight motion of reading agent reasoning, redirecting, and interrupting while the work runs — and the **observability of the middle** that makes steering possible? (See `doctrine/steering.md`.) Beware of changes that quietly make the agent's column more opaque (drop the explain register, hide reasoning behind polished summaries, suppress mid-stream artifacts) — they collapse the gates at the ends into rubber-stamp.
- Does the change preserve **right-sized routing** — the explicit classify-and-route discipline that refuses the agent's frontier-on-every-task default, with open-source / self-hosted as a first-class option? (See `doctrine/right-sized-models.md`.) Beware of changes that silently push work upward in the model stack (removing the classifier, dropping the policy-override rules, treating frontier as the sane default) — they produce default escalation, which is the cost-axis sibling of rubber-stamp.
- Does the ADLC remain a governed process rather than a vague AI workflow?
- Does the audience model remain modular rather than persona theater?
- Do nine-dimension references remain consistent with `doctrine/nine-dimensions-model.md`?
- Does the change preserve lived answerability rather than reducing Clay's authority to only founder or engineering expertise?
- If older source material is used, has it been translated into current Viable Coder doctrine rather than importing outdated framing?
- Should `doctrine/playbook-lexicon.md` be updated?
- If the change affects how the playbook teaches, does it preserve `doctrine/reader-orientation-pattern.md` and the seed -> bloom -> harvest architecture?
