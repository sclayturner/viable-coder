01 Two Sides, One Practice

Long-term viability, in an AI landscape that won't sit still.

Almost everyone talking about AI-native development right now is talking about how to build software without writing code. Too few are asking whether what we're building today will still hold up in three or five years. Even fewer are asking what it will do to us — cognitively, emotionally, economically — to offload our reasoning to machines, every working day, for a career.

This practice does both. One side is mentorship — getting you, or a protégé inside your company, to real AI-native product ownership and full adoption of an agent-driven development lifecycle. The other side is an ongoing inquiry into whether that career move stays viable as conditions shift — measured across nine dimensions the field doesn't yet have shared language for.

02 Nine Questions Few Are Asking

Becoming viable is one question. Remaining viable is nine.

Three families — about the practitioner, the practice, and the conditions the practice depends on. They're how we measure whether the answers we're shipping today still hold up. None of them are rhetorical.

Practitioner · 4

  • Cognitive Am I getting sharper, or duller?
  • Epistemic Do I still know what I know?
  • Vocational Does this still feel like work I want to do?
  • Identity What is my value in this arrangement?

Practice · 3

  • Differential When everyone can do this, what's my value?
  • Judgment What happens when models exercise my judgment?
  • Temporal Does the time I save actually accrue to me?

Conditions · 2

  • Economic Will this still be profitable when the AI subsidy ends?
  • Ethical Is what I'm doing defensible — to clients, the field, the people downstream?
