# Viable Coder Value Proposition
 
## Overview
 
Viable Coder helps founders, leaders, teams, and emerging builders move from AI-assisted "vibe coding" to durable AI-native product ownership.
 
Led by Clay Turner, a 30-year software engineer and career technical founder, the site teaches people how to seize the opportunity in front of them now: to build, own, and improve real software with AI. But it does not treat today's speed as the whole story. Viable Coder also studies what has to remain intact over time: judgment, cognition, craft, economics, ethics, identity, and the conditions that make this new way of working viable.
 
The work has two halves:
 
### Becoming Viable
 
Practical mentorship, resources, and a playbook for six situations: developing internal talent, reducing SaaS dependency, escaping external dev dependency, dealing with legacy software debt, closing the AI development gap, and crossing the prototype-to-production wall.
 
### Remaining Viable
 
A public inquiry into nine dimensions of long-term viability: cognitive, epistemic, vocational, identity, differential, judgment, temporal, economic, and ethical. The point is not just to adopt AI-native work, but to keep asking whether the practice still holds up as the field changes.
 
## Concise Value Proposition
 
Viable Coder teaches people how to become capable AI-native software builders now, while staying honest about what must remain viable over a career.
 
## Positioning Line
 
From vibe coding to viable coding: practical AI-native product ownership, taught by a technical founder and tested against the long-term questions most people are not asking yet.
 
## Mission Statement
 
To help people and companies build real software capability in the age of AI, without confusing short-term productivity with long-term viability.
