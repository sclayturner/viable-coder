## Ch 1 — A Different Clock

**Promise:** You'll see why winning the opportunity and keeping it are the same job measured on two clocks — and why the second clock is the one almost nobody is watching.
**Doctrine drawn on:** [doctrine/nine-dimensions-model.md](doctrine/nine-dimensions-model.md) §1 (Becoming Viable vs. Remaining Viable — *one stance, two clocks*); [doctrine/organic-intelligence.md](doctrine/organic-intelligence.md) (the same instrument across two clocks); [conceptual-playbook-outline.md](book/outlines/conceptual-playbook-outline.md) §Part 4.1.
**Felt-need rhythm:** Scene (three sections in, you can do the thing — *Becoming*) → Tension (there's a second clock running underneath, in years) → Distinction (every short-clock reading is flattering; the long clock is the one almost nobody watches) → Tool *(none yet — Ch 2 names how to read it; Ch 3 hands you the questions; Ch 4 hands you the strategies)* → Return (becoming viable is this build; remaining viable is every build after).

- **1.1 What three sections built up to.** You can hand a goal to a crew of agents (Section 3), hold the two jobs at the ends (Section 1), and run a real build start to finish — your situation already configured (Section 2). That's *Becoming* viable: the short clock, this build. *Key phrase (lifted from [doctrine/nine-dimensions-model.md](doctrine/nine-dimensions-model.md) §1):* "**Becoming Viable converts the AI opportunity into owned capability now, on the short clock of this build.**"
- **1.2 The second clock no one is watching.** A clock that ticks in years. The question on it isn't *can I build this?* — it's *will I still be worth anything as a builder a decade from now — sharp, trusted, and paid?* The career-scale question this whole section exists for. *Key phrase (lifted from §1):* "**Remaining Viable never stops asking what is under threat across the years a career actually runs.**"
- **1.3 The trap of the short clock.** Every reading on it is flattering. A great week, a shipped feature, a happy client — none of it tells you whether the slow things (your judgment, your edge, your reasons for doing this at all) are gaining or quietly draining. The same self-flattery problem Section 1 named, now operating at career scale.
- **1.4 Same stance, longer.** *Key phrase (proposed, from outline Ch 1):* "**one instrument — you — pointed at this build, and kept fit across every build.**" The faculties that win the opportunity are the exact ones you have to keep in form to hold onto it. The maintenance trilogy from Section 1 (condition, observability, upkeep) was always going to land here.
- **1.5 What this section does.** Turns the long clock into nine plain questions you can actually read — and hands you a *menu of strategies* for each, instead of one right answer. The reveal of the section's offer, with the playbook posture surfaced from the start.
- **1.6 The takeaway.** *Key phrase (lifted from outline Ch 1):* "**becoming viable is this build; remaining viable is every build after. Same you, longer clock.**"

**Narrative anchor activated:** *Vision or blind ambition* (returns at career scale — what happens to judgment when the productive friction of being wrong disappears over years, not days); *The dashboard / watchdog* (seeded — the daily watchdog from Section 3 has a career-scale sibling).
**Seeds forward:** Ch 2 (how the long-clock questions are answered without sliding into self-grading); Ch 3 (the nine questions named); Ch 4 (the menu of strategies for each); Ch 5 (you already built this instrument in Section 3 — Section 4 just changes the zoom).
**Editorial note:** Ch 1 is the chapter that names the second clock and the *Becoming / Remaining* split. Both are core doctrine vocabulary from [doctrine/nine-dimensions-model.md](doctrine/nine-dimensions-model.md) §1 and should be used as italicized first-introduction terms.

## Ch 2 — Questions You Can't Lie To

**Promise:** You'll learn why the long-game questions are trends, not a test — and why the answers come from your work, not your gut.
**Doctrine drawn on:** [doctrine/nine-dimensions-model.md](doctrine/nine-dimensions-model.md) §3 (the measurement philosophy — *trend not snapshot;* read against own baseline; honesty ceiling) and §4 (the Auto/Asked mechanism); [doctrine/observability.md](doctrine/observability.md) (instrument-don't-introspect, ported from Section 1 Ch 5 — now at career scale); [conceptual-playbook-outline.md](book/outlines/conceptual-playbook-outline.md) §Part 4.2.
**Felt-need rhythm:** Scene (Section 1's mirror problem, now at career scale) → Tension (a once-a-year *am I still sharp?* gets the most flattering answer available every time) → Distinction (trends, not snapshots; readings, not self-grading; Auto + Asked) → Tool (the Auto/Asked mechanism with cadences) → Return (most honest readings are the ones you don't get to grade).

- **2.1 The mirror problem at career scale.** *Key phrase (S1 Ch 5):* "**the broken part is the part doing the checking.**" That problem doesn't shrink over a career — it grows. Ask once a year *am I still sharp?* and you'll get the most flattering answer available every single time. The doctrine's drunk-driver framing made career-shaped: *the impairment and your read on the impairment fail together, and the failure compounds over years.*
- **2.2 So none of these are snapshot questions.** They are **trend** questions — read over months and years, off a string of readings, not a feeling on a given afternoon. *Key phrase (lifted from [doctrine/nine-dimensions-model.md](doctrine/nine-dimensions-model.md) §3):* "**a bad day is weather and is ignored; a line that bends the wrong way for two quarters is climate and is worth changing course over.**"
- **2.3 The two kinds of readings — Auto.** Most readings your work hands you without your help — call these the **automatic** ones (the doctrine's `Auto` label). How often you reshaped an agent's plan versus rubber-stamped it. How your pricing moved. How your token bill moved. How you described your own work in your last ten bios. These sit in your transcripts, your repo, your calendar, your invoices already. *Key phrase (proposed, from outline Ch 2):* "**you can't fudge them, because you're not the one taking them.**"
- **2.4 The two kinds of readings — Asked.** A few questions genuinely need *you* to answer — the **asked** ones (`Asked`). *Could you still explain, cold, a system you built three months ago? Did this week's work leave you lit up or hollowed out?* You answer in the lightest form the question allows — a one-line note at session's end, a longer walk-through now and then, an essay once a year. *Key phrase (proposed, from outline Ch 2):* "**that answer joins the pile and gets read as a trend like everything else. An asked answer isn't trusted self-report; it's self-report turned into a tracked reading.**" The Asked-becomes-Auto conversion is the load-bearing move.
- **2.5 Cadences — slow questions get slow answers.** Some readings come every session, some weekly, some once a year — and the slow ones aren't lazy. *Key phrase (proposed, from outline Ch 2):* "**an annual question deserves an annual answer; you don't take your career's temperature hourly.**" The cadences map from [doctrine/nine-dimensions-model.md](doctrine/nine-dimensions-model.md) §4 (`Per Session` → `Weekly` → `Monthly` → `Quarterly` → `Per Release` → `Annual`) are doctrine-level.
- **2.6 Read against your own baseline, never against a crowd.** Your normal isn't anyone else's. The Operating Profile from Section 2 Ch 2 set the zero-point each trend gets scored against. *Key phrase (lifted from [doctrine/nine-dimensions-model.md](doctrine/nine-dimensions-model.md) §3):* "**a novelty-driven originator's healthy numbers look alarming on a generic chart and fine against his own baseline.**" The idiographic principle — *instrument the constraint, don't type the person* — held to.
- **2.7 The honesty ceiling, said plainly.** *Key phrase (lifted from outline Ch 2):* "**this is a way to read yourself against your own past, not a graded test with a right answer. It tells you which way you're drifting. It doesn't stamp a number on your worth.**" The line carried so the inquiry never has to apologize for what it isn't — a validated psychometric instrument. *(Outline open decision #6: hold this line as firmly as the research streams hold it.)*
- **2.8 The takeaway.** *Key phrase (lifted from outline Ch 2):* "**trends, not snapshots — and the most honest readings are the ones you don't get to grade.**"

**Narrative anchor activated:** *The dashboard / watchdog* (full bloom on the long clock — what Section 1 wired for state and Section 3 wired for the work, Section 4 reads in years).
**Seeds forward:** Ch 3 (the nine questions, each carried with its Auto/Asked mix); Ch 4 (strategies per dimension that respond to the Auto/Asked readings Ch 3 names); Ch 5 (you already built the substrate in Section 3 — same readings, different zoom); Ch 6 (the modeled heeding — the demonstrations there pick from the Ch 4 menu against actual readings); Ch 7 (the same instrument runs at two scales — your self-study, optionally extended to the cohort).
**Clay-experience anchor slot:** *The trend that beat the gut.* A time a slow reading — taken over months or years, not a bad day — told Clay something his in-the-moment sense had been actively hiding. The chapter's load-bearing scene that proves trend-over-snapshot. Left as a named slot, not written. *(Outline anchor list.)*

## Ch 3 — The Nine Questions, in Three Families

**Promise:** The nine things actually under threat over a career — grouped so you can see what each one is really about, and how you'd read it.
**Doctrine drawn on:** [doctrine/nine-dimensions-model.md](doctrine/nine-dimensions-model.md) §5 (the canonical model — three families; nine dimensions; full signal tables; corpus cross-references) and §6 (master reference table — including the *signal mix* and *corpus seed* columns); [book/modules/relocation-ledger.md](book/modules/relocation-ledger.md) (the eight situation-seeded dimensions and the *Vocational is harvest-forward* note); [conceptual-playbook-outline.md](book/outlines/conceptual-playbook-outline.md) §Part 4.3.
**Felt-need rhythm:** Scene (every reader carries the same fear — *what if I'm gaining capability while quietly losing something I needed?*) → Tension (the fear is too big to act on as one question — and any one piece of it is the wrong frame) → Distinction (three families, nine questions — each with what's under threat and how it's read) → Tool (each question with example reading from §5; full signal tables in the doctrine reference) → Return (every question was planted earlier in this book; here is where they come due — and the next chapter hands strategies for each).

**Structural rule for this chapter.** Each dimension below carries the same five things: the plain-language question, the formal name in parentheses, what's under threat, one or two example readings with their Auto/Asked label, and the corpus cross-reference. The **exhaustive signal tables** — every Auto/Asked label, every cadence, every signal per dimension — live in [doctrine/nine-dimensions-model.md](doctrine/nine-dimensions-model.md) §5 and on the live `remaining-viable.html` page; this granular does not duplicate them. Outline open decision #2 — *keep the full tables out of the book; point to the inquiry pages* — is honored here. **Ch 3 names what's under threat and how to read it; Ch 4 lays out plausible strategies in response (plural per dimension); Ch 6 carries the harder question of whether you actually heed what you see, modeled on one or two of Clay's own readings — the *mitigation* layer and the *heeding* layer are now structurally distinct.**

### Family I — Is the *person* still intact? *(Practitioner)*

- **3.1 Am I getting sharper, or going dull? (Cognitive)** Under threat: the thinking that built your judgment in the first place, and the focus to hold a hard problem in your head for an hour, as you hand more of the work to the model. *Read it (sample):* automatically, from how much of the reasoning in your transcripts is yours versus the agent's, and how often you accept, edit, or reject what it hands you; once a year, asked — could you still solve a fixed set of problems with the AI switched off? *Signal mix (from §6):* **6 Auto · 3 Asked** — the largest mix in the model. *Corpus seed:* Section 1's *you-are-the-infrastructure*; the personal dashboard; Section 2's Internal Talent situation (team-scale version: *is my bench getting stronger or quietly going dull?*).
- **3.2 Do I actually understand what I shipped? (Epistemic)** Under threat: whether you *know* a system the way you'd know code you wrote by hand, when it got built through a conversation with an agent. *Read it (sample):* asked, monthly — explain a system you built 30–90 days ago, cold, no agent; draw its shape from memory and check it against the truth. Automatically — how much of its documentation you wrote versus the agent. *Signal mix:* **1 Auto · 3 Asked.** *Corpus seed:* Section 2's AI Development Gap ("ships what it can't explain"); Section 3's G7 review gate, only honorable if the human has the condition to hold it.
- **3.3 Does this still feel like work I want to do? (Vocational)** Under threat: whether the work still pulls you, as it shifts from solving problems to steering and reviewing. *Key phrase (lifted from [doctrine/nine-dimensions-model.md](doctrine/nine-dimensions-model.md) §5):* "**the dimension most likely to be dismissed as soft — and the one most likely to predict who quietly leaves the field over the next decade.**" *Read it (sample):* asked, weekly and yearly — what are you noticing about the texture of the work; does a session leave you charged or drained; would you want to work exactly this way for another ten years. *Signal mix:* **1 Auto · 3 Asked.** *Corpus seed:* **harvest-forward — no Section 2 situation seeds it** (the relocation ledger's known gap from Section 2 Ch 7.4 and from this section's planning). **Special handling** (per [doctrine/nine-dimensions-model.md](doctrine/nine-dimensions-model.md) §5): separate, granular consent — the reflection journal underlying Vocational tracking is the one stream on the cohort's *separate, optional consent track*. **Vocational needs a Clay-experience anchor more than any other dimension** since the corpus doesn't otherwise build toward it.
- **3.4 When the machine builds it, what's still mine? (Identity)** Under threat: the sense that what you bring is meaningful — that you can stand behind the work with pride when an agent wrote the code and you steered. *Read it (sample):* automatically, from how you describe your own contribution in bios and bylines and transcripts — *"I built," "I orchestrated," "I shipped"* — and which role you speak from. Asked, once a year — what do you actually believe the human adds now. *Signal mix:* **3 Auto · 1 Asked.** *Corpus seed:* Section 1's two jobs (origination and answerability — *am I still a builder if the machine does the producing?*); Section 2's Internal Talent trade lights Identity alongside Cognitive.

### Family II — Is what I *sell* still worth selling? *(Practice)*

- **3.5 When everyone can do this, what's the value of *me* doing it? (Differential)** Under threat: the slice of what you're paid that comes from being *early*, not from being *good.* The early-adopter premium is, by definition, temporary. *Read it (sample):* automatically — how your pricing moves for comparable work; whether prospects bring you *help me start* questions or harder *what next* ones; how fast your distinctive language spreads into everyone else's mouths. *Signal mix:* **3 Auto · 1 Asked.** *Corpus seed:* Section 2's External Dev Dependency situation — *once everyone has the same agents, what's left that's yours.*
- **3.6 What happens when the models do the judgment I sell? (Judgment)** Under threat: the architectural, strategic, and design judgment that AI accelerates today but doesn't yet originate — and that is migrating into the models faster than anyone has reckoned with. *Read it (sample):* on a locked set of hard problems, how the gap between your call and the frontier model's call moves with each new release; each week, the three calls you made that the model wouldn't have. *Signal mix:* **3 Auto · 2 Asked.** *Corpus seed:* Section 2's Legacy Software Debt situation — *how much of the judgment about this code is still mine.*
- **3.7 Does the time I "save" actually come back to me? (Temporal)** Under threat: the whole promise — that the hours AI gives back become *yours.* Every past productivity boom suggests the dividend gets eaten by rising expectations instead of reclaimed. *Read it (sample):* automatically — how your hours split between reactive and generative, whether your total weekly hours are climbing; asked, quarterly — how many hours did you actually take back, and what did you do with them? *Signal mix:* **3 Auto · 1 Asked.** *Corpus seed:* Section 2's SaaS Vendor Dependency situation — *did the savings show up, or get eaten by upkeep?*

### Family III — Is the *ground* still ground? *(Conditions)*

- **3.8 Will what's profitable today still be profitable tomorrow? (Economic)** Under threat: the unit economics of the whole way of working, currently subsidized by investors chasing market share. What happens when the subsidy ends and the real cost meets the list price. *Read it (sample):* automatically — your token cost per unit of output; your margin with AI tooling as its own line; your dependencies' prices month over month; the signals providers send (rate limits, deprecations, new enterprise tiers). *Signal mix:* **4 Auto · 0 Asked** — the only fully-Auto dimension in the model. *Corpus seed:* the cost side of every Section 2 trade — **lit twice** in the relocation ledger (External Dev + SaaS Vendor), the most-pointed-to dimension after Cognitive. *Now also lit operationally by [Section 3 Ch 5 / `doctrine/right-sized-models.md`](book/outlines/section-3-outline.md) — the classify-and-route discipline that refuses the agent's frontier-on-every-task default produces the long-clock readings this dimension reads (frontier-share of output, cost-per-unit-per-tier, override-up and override-down rates, rate-limit incidence, self-hosted utilization). The day-to-day discipline emits the Auto signals; the long clock reads their trends; and Ch 4's Economic strategy menu carries the discipline as its first entry.*
- **3.9 Is what I'm doing defensible — to myself, my clients, the field, and the people downstream? (Ethical)** Under threat: whether the practice can answer for itself across every register it touches — *the environmental cost of the compute, the humans you used to pay and no longer do, work whose origins you can't trace attributed to you, decision power pooling inside three or four labs, and code you didn't write shipped to people you'll never meet.* (The five registers from [doctrine/nine-dimensions-model.md](doctrine/nine-dimensions-model.md) §5 — *and the list keeps growing.*) *Read it (sample):* automatically — how you disclose AI's hand in your work; what fraction of what you ship is machine-made; whether you reach for the smaller model when it would do. Asked, once a year — where you actually stand. *Signal mix:* **8 Auto · 3 Asked** — the densest signal set in the model. *Key phrase (lifted from §5):* "**the practice doesn't get to call itself ethical while the people downstream don't yet agree.**" *Corpus seed:* Section 2's Prototype-to-Production Wall — *accountability in registers a prototype never owed.* *Concentration-of-power register operationally addressed by [Section 3 Ch 5 / `doctrine/right-sized-models.md`](book/outlines/section-3-outline.md):* the open-source / self-hosted-as-first-class-option posture is the day-to-day discipline that refuses lab concentration as a silent dependency. *Frontier-share of output* (from Ch 5's routing signals) reads the concentration-of-power register on the long clock — pairs with the Auto signal "whether you reach for the smaller model when it would do." Two doctrines, same axis. Ch 4's Ethical strategy menu carries both registers as candidate strategies.

- **3.10 The takeaway.** *Key phrase (lifted from outline Ch 3):* "**three families — the person, the practice, the ground. Nine questions. Every one of them was planted earlier in this book; here is where they come due — and the next chapter hands you strategies for each.**" The harvest line — names the seed → bloom → harvest pedagogy explicitly, with the bridge into Ch 4's strategies catalog.

**Narrative anchor activated:** *The dependency trade* (final bloom — each situation's trade from Section 2 returns as the dimension that trade lights up); *Scarcity to capability* (returns across Family III — what was once a constraint becomes the question of whether the conditions hold); *The two gates* (Identity and Vocational both turn on whether the two jobs from Section 1 are still felt as meaningful work).
**Seeds forward:** Ch 4 (the strategies menu for each dimension — what the reader could plausibly try); Ch 5 (your existing Section 3 board reads these on the daily clock — same instrument, different zoom); Ch 6 (the un-delegable move from seeing to heeding — and one or two Clay demonstrations of choosing among the Ch 4 menu); Ch 7 (the cohort scale extends each of the nine readings — opt-in, granular).

## Ch 4 — Strategies for the Nine

**Promise:** A menu of strategies for each dimension — multiple per dimension, drawn from Clay's practice, the field's existing approaches, and reasonable experimentation — presented as patterns to evaluate against your own readings and adapt to your own situation. Not the right moves. Moves worth considering.
**Doctrine drawn on:** [doctrine/nine-dimensions-model.md](doctrine/nine-dimensions-model.md) §5 (per-dimension readings — each strategy is a response to one or more of those readings) and §10 (*Remaining feeds Becoming* — the loop the strategies sit inside); [doctrine/right-sized-models.md](doctrine/right-sized-models.md) (the Economic and Ethical concentration-of-power strategies carry the routing discipline from Section 3 Ch 5 as their first entry); [doctrine/voice/lived-answerability.md](doctrine/voice/lived-answerability.md) (the menu's posture: patterns to evaluate, not answers to follow); the section-1, 2, and 3 outlines and granulars as sources for cross-references in *where it fits* lines.
**Felt-need rhythm:** Scene (the reader has just met the nine threats; the obvious next question is *what do I do?*) → Tension (the temptation is one right answer per dimension; the playbook's posture refuses that) → Distinction (strategies plural per dimension, evaluated against your own readings, adapted to your own situation) → Tool (catalog by dimension, each strategy in the same four-line shape — *reading it addresses, what the move is, where it fits, watchpoint*) → Return (this is a menu; the reader chooses, the playbook doesn't choose for them — and the demonstrations in Ch 6 show what choosing actually looks like).

- **4.1 The shape of a strategy.** *Key phrase (proposed):* "**reading it addresses, what the move is, where it fits, watchpoint.**" The four-line shape kept short on purpose so the reader can scan across strategies within a dimension and across dimensions. The *watchpoint* line is the load-bearing one — it's what keeps a strategy from being run as ritual. *(Editorial test at draft: a strategy without a real watchpoint is a slogan, not a strategy.)*
- **4.2 What this chapter is not.** *Key phrase (proposed, this chapter's posture):* "**not the right moves — moves worth considering.**" Not a prescription. Not a curriculum the reader is meant to complete. The honesty ceiling from Ch 2.7 holds here too: *you read these against your own situation, not against a model practitioner's checklist.* If a strategy is obviously not a fit, skip it. If all of a dimension's strategies look wrong for your situation, the right move may be one you originate yourself — and the chapter's four-line shape gives you the template for writing your own.
- **4.3 How the menu is sourced.** *Key phrase (proposed):* "**some are things I run or have run, some are reasonable field strategies the chapter names plainly, and a few are originated here because the dimension didn't have a well-established response at the right altitude.**" Each entry is **provenance-labeled** at draft time — *from my practice* / *from the field* / *originated here* — so the reader can read across the menu with that knowledge. The labeling is part of how the chapter avoids reading as Clay's prescriptions in disguise. *(Outline open decision #8.)*

### Family I — Strategies for the person

- **4.4 Cognitive — keeping the muscle in use.** Three candidate strategies.
  - **The agent-off slice.** *Reading it addresses:* reframe-vs-accept rate creeping toward accept. *Move:* a scheduled block of work each week where the model is reached for only after solving by hand. *Fits:* practitioners whose acceptance rate has crept up under deadline pressure. *Watchpoint:* if the slice keeps getting bumped for "real work," the slice *was* the real work. *(Provenance: Clay-marked — current practice.)*
  - **The annual hard-problem benchmark.** *Reading it addresses:* the annual *could you still solve a fixed set of problems with AI off* Asked reading from §5. *Move:* a personal set of 5–10 problems re-solved annually with AI off, tracking how long they take. *Fits:* anyone who wants a falsifiable Asked reading. *Watchpoint:* if you skip a year, the next year's reading isn't a trend, it's a snapshot. *(Provenance: from the field — established practice in deliberate-practice literature, ported.)*
  - **The reshape-don't-accept rule.** *Reading it addresses:* per-PR acceptance pattern. *Move:* a per-PR practice of restating the agent's plan in your own words before approving. *Fits:* practitioners whose acceptance rate has crept up under deadline pressure. *Watchpoint:* if your restatement is "looks good, ship it" rather than substantive reframing, the rule has become ritual. *(Provenance: originated here — derived from the G7 review gate of Section 3 Ch 3.)*
- **4.5 Epistemic — explaining what you shipped.** Three candidate strategies.
  - **The monthly cold walk-through.** *Reading it addresses:* the monthly Asked reading from §5 — explain a 30–90 day old system from memory. *Move:* schedule the walk-through monthly, then check against the code. *Fits:* practitioners signing off on agent-built systems at G7. *Watchpoint:* if the memory-vs-truth gap widens consistently, the review gate wasn't real for those builds. *(Provenance: Clay-marked, depth-honest — current watch-point, may not yet be a fully-built monthly practice.)*
  - **Self-authored reason-for-being.** *Reading it addresses:* the Auto reading of how much of a system's documentation you wrote vs. the agent. *Move:* write the *why* and *trade-offs* section of each system's docs yourself, before the agent fills in the rest. *Fits:* practitioners whose docs are entirely agent-generated. *Watchpoint:* if you can't write the section without the agent, you don't yet understand what you shipped. *(Provenance: from the field — established docs-as-code practice, repurposed.)*
  - **The replacement test.** *Reading it addresses:* the same memory-vs-truth gap, at greater depth. *Move:* periodically rebuild a small system by hand, comparing your reconstruction to the agent-built original. *Fits:* practitioners with the capacity for a deliberate slowdown. *Watchpoint:* if you skip the comparison, the test was nostalgia. *(Provenance: originated here.)*
- **4.6 Vocational — protecting what still pulls.** Three candidate strategies.
