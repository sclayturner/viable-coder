## Ch 1 — This Book Doesn't Have One Reader

**Promise:** Stop reading a generic playbook and start getting the one built for where you actually are.
**Doctrine drawn on:** [conceptual-playbook-outline.md](book/outlines/conceptual-playbook-outline.md) §Part 2.1; [organic-intelligence.md](doctrine/organic-intelligence.md) (the two ends as the sorting key).
**Felt-need rhythm:** Scene (Section 1 was the same for everyone) → Tension (the two jobs don't change, but *which one you drop* does) → Distinction (modular, not generic) → Tool *(none here — Ch 2 administers the check)* → Return (six situations + the freedom to borrow).

- **1.1 What Section 1 fixed for everyone.** The two jobs, the wager, the instrument — the same for every reader. Restated in one sentence so the reader feels the shared floor before it splits.
- **1.2 Where Section 1 stops.** The two jobs don't change. *Which one you tend to drop* does — and so does the fix. *(Proposed phrase: "**the two jobs are universal; the way you drop one isn't.**")*
- **1.3 The modular premise.** A book with one reader teaches one move. A book with the *actual* readers teaches one set of moves with the right configuration for each. *Key phrase (from §Part 2.1):* "**interchangeable building blocks of guidance + exercises.**"
- **1.4 Six common spots, plus permission to borrow.** Six situations get a default kit; any reader borrows freely from the others where life calls for it. The promise stated.
- **1.5 Why the modularity is invisible to you (and that's the point).** The reader walks a linear path; the configuration happens behind the scenes — the *situation* picks the module set, the *persona* sets develop-vs-borrow, and the *wiring* tunes delivery. *Key phrase (from [module-skill-crosswalk.md](book/modules/module-skill-crosswalk.md) §The governing idea):* "**situation picks the module set; persona sets develop-vs-borrow; wiring tunes the delivery.**"

**Narrative anchor activated:** *The dependency trade* (introduced as the thing Section 2 will earn the reader the right to see).
**Seeds forward:** Ch 2 (the check that routes you); Ch 3 (the develop/borrow call per skill); Section 3 (the same kit becomes the setup sheet for the ADLC).
**Editorial note:** Ch 1 routes. No diagnostic yet, no six situations named, no relocation reframe — those land in their own chapters.

## Ch 2 — The Check That Points You to Your Spot

**Promise:** A short, honest read on where you stand — one that works even though you can't be trusted to grade yourself.
**Doctrine drawn on:** [observability.md](doctrine/observability.md) (instrument-don't-introspect, ported from Section 1 Ch 5); [audience-research-plan.md](book/modules/audience-research-plan.md) §Stream C (the diagnostic) + §Stream F (the operating profile).
**Felt-need rhythm:** Scene (the same mirror that lied in Section 1) → Tension (a self-rating can't pick your situation honestly either) → Distinction (read behavior, not feelings) → Tool (the Two Ends Diagnostic + the Operating Profile, administered as one intake) → Return (a starting point, not a label).

- **2.1 The mirror problem returns.** The same trap Section 1 Ch 5 named, now pointed at situation. *Key phrase (S1 Ch 5):* "**The broken part is the part doing the checking.**" If you grade your own situation, you flatter the work you've been doing.
- **2.2 What the check refuses to ask.** Not "how good are you" — a verdict you'd issue about yourself. Not "what do you wish were true." The flattering question is the one we already ruled out.
- **2.3 What it reads instead.** Behavior: *the last thing you built, the last thing you shipped, the last thing you inherited.* Behavioral evidence over self-rating. *(Stream C: "**self-assessment instrument design that resists the flattering answer (forced-choice, behavioral evidence over self-rating).**")*
- **2.4 The first read — which end you hold badly.** The producible/answerable split as the sorting key, ported from Section 1 Ch 3. *Key phrase:* "**which end do you hold badly?**" The answer routes you to one of the six situations and to its default kit.
- **2.5 The second read — how you're wired.** Not how you *feel* you work; how you actually work. Pacing, scaffolding density, novelty load, single-threading the dense calls. *(Stream F.)* *Key phrase (proposed seed for S1):* "**the instrument came with a factory configuration; know it before you tune it.**"
- **2.6 Weather vs. climate.** Section 1 instrumented your *weather* — the day-to-day variance. This reads your *climate* — the stable wiring underneath. *Key phrase (Stream F):* "**ADHD creative and a methodical sequential thinker have different climates, and the same bad night degrades each differently.**"
- **2.7 The methodological line that protects this.** *Key phrase (Stream F):* "**Functional, not categorical — instrument the constraint, don't type the person.**" Not a personality test; not a learning-style theory. A read on configuration, used to tune delivery.
- **2.8 What comes out.** A *starting point*, not a label. A situation (which kit loads), a persona tag (develop-vs-borrow), and a wiring profile (delivery pacing). Re-take as you change.
- **2.9 The composition that runs the rest of the book.** *Key phrase (carried from Ch 1.5):* "**situation picks the module set; persona sets develop-vs-borrow; wiring tunes the delivery.**" The check is what selects all three.

**Narrative anchor activated:** *The dashboard / watchdog* (returns as the intake instrument; the same observability principle pointed at situation instead of state).
**Seeds forward:** Ch 3 (the develop-vs-borrow read becomes the per-skill call); Ch 4 (the check itself is one of the universals); Section 3 (the check's three outputs configure the ADLC's interaction layer — the crosswalk's join).
**Open structural question:** The crosswalk lists the *Two Ends Diagnostic* and the *Operating Profile read* as two of the five universal modules administered together at intake. Section 1's outline does not currently seed either one. **Decide:** does Section 1 need a one-line seed of each (per Streams E and F in the research plan), or does Section 2 introduce them cold?

## Ch 3 — Build It, or Borrow It — Knowing Which

**Promise:** The single call that saves you the most wasted effort — for each skill, whether to own it or just direct it.
**Doctrine drawn on:** [organic-intelligence.md](doctrine/organic-intelligence.md) (the two ends, applied per skill); [audience-research-plan.md](book/modules/audience-research-plan.md) §How this plan is organized (the load-bearing diagnostic); [conceptual-playbook-outline.md](book/outlines/conceptual-playbook-outline.md) §Part 2.2.
**Felt-need rhythm:** Scene (every skill carries the same question) → Tension (you can get it wrong both ways) → Distinction (develop the faculty vs. borrow the frame) → Tool (the per-skill call) → Return (today's borrow can become tomorrow's build).

- **3.1 Back to the two jobs, narrowed to one skill.** The front (origination) and the back (answerability) restated for the move in front of you — not for your career, for *this* skill.
- **3.2 The question, per skill.** *Key phrase (lifted from outline Ch 3):* "**do you need to *own* this, or just *direct* it well enough to stay in charge?**" The sharpest sorting question in the section.
- **3.3 The two reads it produces.** *Key phrase (load-bearing, from [audience-research-plan.md](book/modules/audience-research-plan.md)):* "**develop the faculty**" (you want this end as a real, owned capability) vs. "**borrow the frame**" (you need just enough to stay answerable for a call you'll never author).
- **3.4 Build for real when it's yours to live with.** When the skill is downstream of consequences you'll personally hold — for years, not weeks.
- **3.5 Borrow just enough when you'll always hand it off.** *Key phrase (proposed):* "**enough frame to stay answerable for a call you will never author.**"
- **3.6 The trap on each side.** Borrow when you should have built → *you go soft. You can't catch a problem you don't understand.* Build when you should have borrowed → *you sink weeks into something you could have rented.* Both fail the two-jobs test; they fail at different ends.
- **3.7 The persona overlay.** The same situation, two different readers, two different defaults. A first-time founder building origination as a real faculty; a repeat founder borrowing the engineering frame to stay credible directing. *(Surfacing the persona dimension the conceptual outline §2.3 flags as the cross-cutting overlay.)*
- **3.8 Borrowing now can turn into building later.** *Key phrase (lifted from outline Ch 3):* "**borrowing now can turn into building later.**" The decision is reversible by intent — start with just enough, grow into it as the skill becomes yours.
- **3.9 Why this distinction also calibrates the agents.** Foreshadow: the same diagnostic that picks your move also configures the AI's teaching register in Section 3 — *transfer fully* (Explanatory) for the develop-the-faculty reader, *produce-with-minimum-frame* for the borrow reader. *Key phrase (from the crosswalk):* "**Write each Skill once, with both registers as switchable modes; select the mode by persona tag at intake — never maintain two Skill libraries.**"

**Narrative anchor activated:** *The two gates* (the two ends, now applied at the skill level rather than the career level).
**Seeds forward:** Ch 4 (universals don't ask this question — they're held by everyone); Section 3 Ch 5 ("Teaching the AI Your Way" — the register knob is set by this chapter's call); Section 3 Ch 3 (the same E⇄P switch on the per-gate matrix).
**Open structural question:** Ch 2 already reads develop-vs-borrow at intake (as the *persona tag*); Ch 3 unpacks the per-skill version of the same call. **Decide:** is the two-beat rhythm load-bearing (Ch 2 reads it at the level of *you*; Ch 3 applies it at the level of *each skill*), or are these one chapter?

## Ch 4 — The Moves Everyone Makes

**Promise:** Five moves that go in every kit, whoever you are — because they protect the two jobs no matter your situation.
**Doctrine drawn on:** [conceptual-playbook-outline.md](book/outlines/conceptual-playbook-outline.md) §Design Principles; [organic-intelligence.md](doctrine/organic-intelligence.md); [human-condition.md](doctrine/human-condition.md); [module-skill-crosswalk.md](book/modules/module-skill-crosswalk.md) §The five universals.
**Felt-need rhythm:** Scene (whatever your situation, five moves go in every kit) → Tension (situation-specific kits without universals leave the universal failure modes uncovered) → Distinction (universals hold the ends; situation modules configure the middle) → Tool (the five moves) → Return (the floor; situation adds the rest on top).

- **4.1 Why some moves belong to everyone.** The two jobs are universal; the moves that protect them are universal too. Situation configures the middle; universals hold the ends.
- **4.2 Move 1 — Did I choose this, or did it choose me?** The first question of the book, now pointed at the work in front of you. *Key phrase (S1 Ch 3):* "**Did I choose this — or did it choose me?**" *(Crosswalk universal: Can-vs-Should Gate.)*
- **4.3 Move 2 — You are the infrastructure.** From Section 1, restated as a kit-level universal. Your condition shapes every call you make here. *Key phrase (S1 Ch 4):* "**You are the infrastructure.**" *(Crosswalk universal: You-Are-Infrastructure.)*
- **4.4 Move 3 — Every fix is a trade.** No move makes a problem vanish; it swaps it for a new one. The skill isn't escaping problems — it's *picking the one worth having.* The Section 2 bloom of the spine's stake asymmetry: *you bear all the risk; you can choose its shape.* *Key phrase (proposed, from outline Ch 4):* "**every fix is a trade.**" *(Crosswalk universal: Relocation Lens.)*
- **4.5 Move 4 — Stay able to check work you didn't write.** The AI writes faster than anyone can read. Guard the one thing that doesn't speed up: your ability to actually review it. *Key phrase (proposed, from outline Ch 4):* "**guard the one thing that doesn't speed up: your ability to actually review it.**"
- **4.6 Move 5 — Say what "right" means before you build.** Write the target down first, so you can tell whether the AI hit it or just gave you something that *looks finished.* *Key phrase (proposed, from outline Ch 4):* "**say what 'right' means before you build.**"
- **4.7 The takeaway.** *Key phrase (lifted from outline):* "**these five are the floor. Your situation adds the rest on top.**"

**Narrative anchor activated:** *The dependency trade* (Move 3 is the kit-level statement of the anchor that blooms fully in Ch 5).
**Seeds forward:** Ch 5 (each situation's relocation ledger is Move 3 instantiated); Section 3 Ch 4 ("The Gates You Can Never Hand Off" — Moves 4 and 5 become structural gates G7 and G2); Section 4 (Move 3's "trade" becomes the dimension lit by the situation, harvested across years).
**Open structural question:** The outline's five moves and the crosswalk's five universal modules **overlap but are not identical**. Outline: *(1) Did I choose this, (2) You are the infrastructure, (3) Every fix is a trade, (4) Stay able to check work you didn't write, (5) Say what "right" means before you build.* Crosswalk: *Two Ends Diagnostic, Operating Profile, Relocation Lens, You-Are-Infrastructure, Can-vs-Should.* Three overlap cleanly (Moves 1, 2, 3 ↔ Can-vs-Should, You-Are-Infrastructure, Relocation Lens). The Two Ends Diagnostic and Operating Profile are *intake* universals (administered in Ch 2). Moves 4 and 5 are *ongoing-discipline* universals (no intake equivalent). **Decide:** call these "**intake universals**" (Ch 2) vs. "**ongoing universals**" (Ch 4) explicitly, or treat the two lists as one set with two homes?

## Ch 5 — Find Your Situation

**Promise:** The six most common spots people are stuck in — find yours, and meet the kit built for it.
**Doctrine drawn on:** [audience-research-plan.md](book/modules/audience-research-plan.md) §The six situations; [relocation-ledger.md](book/modules/relocation-ledger.md) (the master pointer table + the six ledgers); [conceptual-playbook-outline.md](book/outlines/conceptual-playbook-outline.md) §Part 2.3.
**Felt-need rhythm:** Scene (six common spots) → Tension (which is you? which end held badly? what is the trade?) → Distinction (every escape is a trade — the relocation lens) → Tool (one ledger per situation; the residual-risk signature) → Return (each spot drops a different job and makes a different trade — and the trade is what you'll track for the rest of your career).

### The reframe before the six (the relocation lens, in full)

- **5.1 The instinct everyone arrives with.** Find the fix that *gets rid of* the dependency you're worried about. *(Set up the wrong reading so the right one can land.)*
- **5.2 The reframe.** *Key phrase (from [relocation-ledger.md](book/modules/relocation-ledger.md)):* "**Every escape is a trade.**" *Key phrase (Stream E):* "**no move sheds risk, it only changes risk's shape.**" Follows straight from Section 1's keystone — if the human bears all the risk, the move can only *relocate* it.
- **5.3 The teachable reframe.** *Key phrase (Stream E):* "**escaping dependency was never the goal; choosing which dependency is worth holding is.**" *Origination applied to the dependency itself — Did I choose this, or did it choose me? pointed at the trade.*
- **5.4 The tone the reframe has to land in.** Not nihilism. *Key phrase (Stream E):* "**clear-eyed choosing, not 'it's all dependencies, why bother': you're trading up to a dependency worth holding.**"
- **5.5 The mechanical move that makes the trade real.** *Key phrase (from [relocation-ledger.md](book/modules/relocation-ledger.md)):* "**force at least three entries into the Adopted column, then sign one residual-risk statement.**" *Key phrase:* "**That signature is *Did I choose this, or did it choose me?* pointed at the dependency rather than the product.**" *Key phrase:* "**An empty Adopted column means the trade wasn't decided, just made.**"

### The six situations (each as one beat — full ledgers in [relocation-ledger.md](book/modules/relocation-ledger.md))

Each beat carries the outline's five sub-bullets (*this is you if / the risk / what you do / the trade / the long-game question*) plus the named **end held badly**, the **develop-or-borrow default**, and the **dimension(s) lit** as a Section 4 seed (named only).

- **5.6 ITW — You've got people worth growing.** *Holds both ends; wants to transfer the engineering and product-ownership faculty into specific developable people — an individual standout, or a small team of change-makers (the handful who would shift how the org actually builds, if developed). Not org-wide training; not hiring; not bootcamps.* Default: *develop the faculty, fully.* Long-game question: *"**is my bench actually getting stronger, or quietly going dull?**"* Lights up **Identity · Cognitive.**
- **5.7 SVD — You're renting what you could own.** *Both ends sit with the vendor.* Default: *borrow the frame, minimally.* Long-game question: *"**did the time and money I saved actually show up — or get eaten by the upkeep I took on?**"* Lights up **Temporal · Economic.**
- **5.8 EDD — Someone else builds your software.** *Origination partial, answerability largely external.* Default: *borrow the frame* (founder); *develop the faculty* if there's internal talent to receive it. Long-game question: *"**what makes me different from the next founder with the exact same stack — and does it still pay?**"* Lights up **Differential · Economic.**
- **5.9 LSD — You inherited a mess you're scared to touch.** *Holds the answerable end but can't hold the frame — it's missing, not abdicated.* Default: *develop the faculty.* Long-game question: *"**how much of the judgment about this code is still mine?**"* Lights up **Judgment.**
- **5.10 AID — Your team hasn't really moved on AI, or it has and you can't answer for what it ships.** *Two shapes — sometimes both at once. **Shape A:** stalled adoption / evaluation paralysis / not having started; both ends held invisibly because the team hasn't met the gap. **Shape B:** producible middle flows, answerable back end unheld — verification debt. Same kit reaches both.* Default: *develop the faculty.* Long-game question: *"**can I still make that call as the volume keeps climbing?**"* Lights up **Epistemic · Cognitive.**
- **5.11 PPW — Your demo has to become a real product.** *Built on Lovable / Base44 / Replit / Bubble / a Cursor-vibe stack — chose those tools specifically because they appeared to remove engineering time and cost from the equation. Built it, looks amazing, traction showed up, scaling isn't happening. Origination happened and the thing exists, but the answerable back end was never required by a prototype.* Default: *develop on the non-functional surface; borrow where you'll rely on specialists/agents.* Long-game question: *"**am I ready to be accountable in ways a prototype never asked of me?**"* Lights up **Ethical.**

- **5.12 The takeaway.** *Key phrase (lifted from outline Ch 5):* "**each spot drops a different job and makes a different trade — but the trade is the part you'll be tracking for the rest of your career.**"

**Narrative anchor activated:** *The dependency trade* (full bloom — the anchor's home chapter); *The prototype-to-production wall* (returns as one of the six and as PPW's full ledger).
**Seeds forward:** Ch 6 (real life rarely fits one situation — borrow from the others on purpose); Section 3 (the kit becomes the configuration of the ADLC); Section 4 (each situation's Adopted column *is* what Section 4 measures; each long-game question becomes a tracked dimension).
**Open structural question:** Six situations × roughly six beats each is the densest chapter in the section by far. **Decide:** does Ch 5 ship as one chapter (reader reads only the situation they tested into; the other five sit as a reference), or does each situation become its own short chapter (Ch 5a–5f)? The matrix form ([section-3-chapter-3-matrix.md](book/outlines/section-3-chapter-3-matrix.md)) is a third option — write the chapter once and configure it six ways at the per-beat level. The relocation-ledger already pre-stages much of this.

## Ch 6 — Your Kit Isn't a Cage

**Promise:** The freedom to borrow — so the playbook fits your real life, not just the box you landed in.
**Doctrine drawn on:** [conceptual-playbook-outline.md](book/outlines/conceptual-playbook-outline.md) §Part 2.1 (modularity premise); [audience-research-plan.md](book/modules/audience-research-plan.md) §Module-to-audience mapping (the borrow grid).
**Felt-need rhythm:** Scene (the kit, ready to go) → Tension (real life never fits one box) → Distinction (borrow on purpose, not at random) → Tool (the borrow grid; the two universals everyone takes) → Return (the kit is the floor, not the ceiling).

- **6.1 What your kit hands you.** The starter set built for your spot — modules ready to go. *(Restate the result of Ch 2 + Ch 5.)*
- **6.2 The reality.** *Key phrase (lifted from outline Ch 6):* "**real life rarely fits one box.**" The founder bringing work in-house also inherits a mess; the team closing the AI gap also hits the demo-to-product wall.
- **6.3 The move.** *Key phrase (lifted from outline Ch 6):* "**borrow on purpose: when your reality calls for a move from another situation, pull it in — deliberately, not at random.**"
- **6.4 The two borrows almost everyone needs.** *Key phrase (lifted from outline Ch 6):* "**checking work you didn't write**" and "**saying what 'right' means first.**" *(Note: these are also Moves 4 and 5 from Ch 4 — the chapter recovers the universals if Ch 4 didn't land hard enough; otherwise this is the reminder.)*
- **6.5 How to borrow without losing the spine.** Borrow modules; don't trade your end. The reader who borrows the SVD's *minimum-frame* exercise is still developing the faculty if that's their default — they borrow the *module*, not the *register*.
- **6.6 Borrowing as the bridge between situations.** Situations are not fixed addresses. A reader can move (SVD → ITW as they hire; PPW → AID as the team grows). Borrowing now is how the kit travels with the reader.

**Narrative anchor activated:** *The dependency trade* (returns: borrowing a module is itself a small trade — what you gain in fit, you pay in coherence).
**Seeds forward:** Ch 7 (the first move this week + the bridges forward); Section 3 (the kit is configured but not yet *run* — Section 3 runs it through the ADLC).
**Editorial note:** Ch 6 is short by design. It is a permission chapter, not a doctrine chapter. The work it does is to keep Ch 5 from reading as a label.

## What this granular view surfaces (structural observations)

These are observations the sub-beat view makes visible. Not decisions — things to hold while Section 2 thinking continues.

1. **Two of Section 2's bloomed concepts have no Section 1 seed yet.** The *relocation lens* (Stream E) and the *operating profile* (Stream F) both ask, in the research plan, for "a one-line seed in Section 1's stance." Section 1's current outline and granular have neither. Either Section 1 needs the two seeds added (per the research-plan recommendation), or Section 2 has to introduce them cold and explain the absence.

2. **Section 2 has two families of universals, not one.** Ch 2 administers the *intake universals* (the Two Ends Diagnostic and the Operating Profile read — they're how the check works). Ch 4 names the *ongoing universals* (Did I choose this; You are the infrastructure; Every fix is a trade; Check work you didn't write; Say what "right" means). Three overlap; two are unique to each family. Decide whether to make the two families explicit, or treat the lists as one set with two homes.

3. **Ch 2 and Ch 3 both turn on develop-vs-borrow.** Ch 2 reads it at the level of *you* (the persona tag the intake assigns); Ch 3 applies it at the level of *each skill*. Same call, two scopes. The two-beat felt-need rhythm may be load-bearing (the second time has teeth, with the per-skill stakes named) or it may be redundancy.

4. **Ch 5 is the densest chapter in the section by a wide margin.** Six situations × ~six beats each is roughly 36 sub-beats in one chapter — the relocation-ledger already pre-stages most of them. The chapter could ship as one (with five situations as reference for borrowing); as six small chapters; or via the matrix form (one chapter, six configurations).

5. **The persona overlay is present but mostly invisible.** Ch 2 produces a persona tag; Ch 3 uses it (3.7); Ch 5 is silent about it; Ch 7 doesn't bridge it. The conceptual outline's open decision #2 names "personas vs. situations" as unresolved. The granular view shows that Section 2 currently treats personas as an *internal authoring tag*, not as a reader-facing concept — which may be the right call, but it should be a deliberate one.

6. **Eight of nine dimensions are seeded; Vocational is not.** Per the relocation-ledger's coverage note, the six situations collectively light Identity, Cognitive, Temporal, Economic, Differential, Judgment, Epistemic, and Ethical. **Vocational** is the only Section 4 dimension with no Section 2 pointer. A deliberate Section 4 call (is Vocational genuinely harvest-only?) — but worth noting that Section 2 sets up Section 4 *almost* exhaustively.

7. **The chapter count.** Section 2 has 7 chapters, matching Section 1. If Ch 5 splits into per-situation chapters, the count grows to 12. If Ch 2/Ch 3 compress (per observation #3), it shrinks to 6. The 4×7 symmetry isn't load-bearing.

8. **Section 2 makes Section 3 visible only through the kit.** The crosswalk ([module-skill-crosswalk.md](book/modules/module-skill-crosswalk.md)) does heavy structural work that the reader never sees — module-to-Skill pairing, register switching, signal feeds. Section 2 Ch 7 mentions only "setup sheet." Decide whether the reader needs to see *any* of the join (the develop-the-faculty audiences may benefit from seeing the Skill behind their module; borrow-the-frame audiences probably don't).

## How to use this artifact

- As a **structural read** — scan the sub-beat headers to see the section's shape at a glance.
- As a **phrase ledger** — the quoted phrases are the load-bearing language already in doctrine and the supporting artifacts; new chapter prose can pull from here.
- As a **decision surface** — the open structural questions above are the calls that are still open at the section level.
