# The Nine Dimensions of Long-Term Viability — Model Reference
 
*Canonical specification for Section 4 ("Playing the Long Game") and the* Remaining Viable *inquiry. This document is the single source of truth for the model: the three families, the nine dimensions, what each puts under threat, the questions that read them, and how those questions are answered. The Section 4 outline now defers to this document for the full signal detail it deliberately leaves out, and the lexicon's Section 4 entries should be reconciled to it.*
 
*Derived from the live staged surfaces — `remaining-viable.html` (the model), `research.html` (the naming surface), and `data.html` (the measurement surface) — which are treated as **canonical** wherever they differ from earlier corpus drafts. Where the page text is Clay's first-person prose, this document restructures it into reference form; the measurement questions and their labels are preserved verbatim, because they are the functional specification.*
 
---
 
## 1. The model at a glance
 
*Remaining Viable* is the long-clock half of the site's governing stance — *one stance, two clocks.* **Becoming Viable** converts the AI opportunity into owned capability now, on the short clock of this build. **Remaining Viable** never stops asking what is under threat across the years a career actually runs. The nine dimensions are that long clock made legible.
 
The deeper offer, stated on the page: capability acquisition is not the deepest thing on sale. The deeper thing is whether the practice itself stays viable over time — *written about publicly, and measured where measurement is honest.*
 
Four structural facts define the model:
 
1. **Three families, nine dimensions.** The nine concerns sort into three families — the *Practitioner* (is the person still intact?), the *Practice* (is what I sell still worth selling?), and the *Conditions* (is the ground still ground?). Membership is 4 / 3 / 2.
2. **Every dimension is a trend, not a snapshot.** Each carries a guiding question, a statement of what is under threat, and a set of **signals** read over time — never a point-in-time self-grade.
3. **Every signal is labeled `Auto` or `Asked`, with a cadence.** *Auto* signals are taken off the practitioner's own digital footprint without their intervention; *Asked* signals are the residue that genuinely needs a human answer, given in the lightest form and then folded back into the footprint for analysis.
4. **Two scales, two surfaces.** The instrument runs at two scales — Clay as the primary subject (a self-study in public) and an optional opt-in cohort — and is published across two surfaces: **Research** names what is worth measuring; **Data** measures it. A pre-committed honesty statement binds them.

## 2. Why this exists — the self-study conducted in public
 
The inquiry's first principle is that **Clay is the subject.** These are questions he is working to answer about his own practice — his day-to-day work as technical co-founder of Soaring Titan and Maximum Automotive Intelligence, the same corpus that feeds the site's Resources. The site's phrase for this is *one instrument, two directions:* the readings that sharpen his own practice are the ones he publishes.
 
This framing is load-bearing for credibility. The Data surface states it bluntly: *"Not a dashboard. Not branding. I am running these measurements on myself."* The model is not a framework imposed on a reader; it is a working practitioner instrumenting himself, with the reader invited to do the same if they choose.
 
Two decoupling rules protect that invitation from becoming a sales funnel:
 
- **The mentorship and the inquiry are separate.** A mentee who wants only the mentorship gets only the mentorship — no data sharing, no measurement, no change to how they work. Opting into the inquiry is *never* a condition of being mentored.
- **The measurement surface is not a sales surface.** The Data page carries no call-to-action by design: *"The measurement only works if it isn't also a sales surface."* This firewall is itself part of the model.

## 3. The measurement philosophy (the spine)
 
The whole instrument is a response to one problem, carried forward from Section 1: **self-report lies at the moment you most need it to hold.** The drunk-driver problem — the impairment and your read on the impairment fail together — does not shrink over a career; it compounds. Ask yourself once a year whether you are still sharp and you will get the most flattering answer available every time.
 
Three commitments follow:
 
- **Trend, not snapshot.** The honest reading is the trailing one, taken off behavior over time, not a feeling on a given afternoon. A bad day is weather and is ignored; a line that bends the wrong way for two quarters is climate and is worth changing course over.
- **Read against the individual's own baseline.** Signals are scored as deviation from the person's *own* established normal, not against a population norm — the idiographic principle developed in the Operating Profile stream (the climate read captured at intake is the zero-point the Cognitive dimension, and the rest, are later read against). A novelty-driven originator's healthy numbers look alarming on a generic chart and fine against his own baseline.
- **An honesty ceiling, stated so the inquiry never has to apologize later.** This is a disciplined way to read yourself against your own past, and a public inquiry across a small cohort — *not* a validated psychometric battery. It tells you which way you are drifting; it does not stamp a number on your worth, and it does not claim a validated idiographic factor model. Every research stream in the corpus carries this humility note; the measurement section carries it most.

## 4. How the questions are answered — the Auto / Asked mechanism
 
Every signal carries one of two labels and a cadence (`Auto · Weekly`, `Asked · Annual`, and so on). This is the mechanism that lets even the soft dimensions be read without sliding back into trusted self-report.
 
**`Auto` — taken off the digital footprint, analyzed without further intervention.** The footprint includes: agent transcripts, repositories and git history, calendar and time data, billing, public bios and bylines, provider pricing pages, and field publications. Because the practitioner is not the one taking the reading, an Auto signal cannot be flattered. (*Agent transcript,* per the Data page's definition: the back-and-forth between the practitioner and the AI tools they use, captured the way a recorded meeting yields a transcript for analysis.)
 
**`Asked` — requires a direct answer, in the lightest form the question allows.** The form scales to the question: an agent skill prompting at session close for a per-session reading; a scheduled walkthrough when the question needs more room; a long essay when the scope is annual. The critical move: *once given, an Asked response joins the same footprint and is analyzed downstream like everything else.* An Asked answer is therefore **not** trusted self-report — it is self-report converted into a tracked, trended artifact.
 
**Cadences** range from `Per Session` and `Weekly` through `Monthly`, `Quarterly`, and `Per Release` to `Annual`. Slow questions get slow answers by design — an annual question deserves an annual answer; you do not take a career's temperature hourly.

## 5. The three families and nine dimensions
 
*Each dimension below gives: the guiding question, what is under threat, the full signal set (with `Auto`/`Asked` label and cadence preserved as specified), and the corpus cross-reference — where the concept was first planted under the seed → bloom → harvest pedagogy.*
 
### Family I — Viability of the Practitioner
 
*The family question: am I, the person, still intact?*
 
#### Cognitive Viability
**Question:** Is my cognitive system — capacity, focus, and growth — still intact?
 
**What's under threat:** The thinking that built your judgment in the first place, and the focus to hold a hard problem in your head for an hour. Four risks run in parallel: core capacity atrophying as more of the work is handed to the model; executive function eroding under AI-native conditions; quietly ceasing to pick up the new concepts and vocabulary that have always renewed the craft; and answerable ownership diluting as the count of concurrent systems exceeds what one mind can hold. The gap between systems touched and systems owned is the rate of abdication-by-over-distribution.
 
**Signals — maintenance:**
- Can I still solve a fixed class of problems without AI tools? — `Asked · Quarterly`
- How much of the reasoning in my agent transcripts is self-generated versus AI-generated? — `Auto · Weekly`
- How often am I accepting AI suggestions outright, modifying them, or rejecting them? — `Auto · Weekly`
- How do I score on a credentialed cognitive battery, year over year? — `Asked · Annual`
**Signals — growth and executive function:**
- Are new concepts, patterns, and architectures appearing in my own transcript turns that weren't there before? — `Auto · Monthly`
- Is my own software-engineering vocabulary still expanding in my writing and transcripts? — `Auto · Monthly`
- How many systems am I touching concurrently — commits, PRs, or transcript activity in the past 14 days? — `Auto · Weekly`
- Of those concurrent systems, how many do I feel I actively own? — `Asked · Monthly`
- Is my ability to stay focused — within a session and across a day — improving or eroding? — `Auto · Weekly`
**Corpus cross-reference:** Section 1's "you are the infrastructure"; the personal dashboard of the maintenance trilogy (condition / observability / upkeep). Section 2's Internal-Talent situation asks the team-scale version (is the bench getting stronger or quietly dulling?). The relocation ledger lights Cognitive from both Internal Talent and the AI Development Gap. The flagship trailing signals here — *origination ratio* and *reframe-vs-accept rate* — are the playbook's own, with no off-the-shelf equivalent.
 
#### Epistemic Viability
**Question:** Do I still know what I know?
 
**What's under threat:** Whether you still actually understand what you shipped. When a system is built through dialogue with an agent, do you know it the way you knew code you wrote by hand?
 
**Signals:**
- Can I explain, cold, a system I built 30–90 days ago — without agent access? — `Asked · Monthly`
- How quickly can I debug a prior-built system without the agent's help? — `Asked · Quarterly`
- How much of the documentation on my shipped systems was written by me versus by the agent? — `Auto · Monthly`
- Can I draw the architecture of one of my systems from memory — and does it match ground truth? — `Asked · Quarterly`
**Corpus cross-reference:** Section 2's "ships what it can't explain" situation (the AI Development Gap; verification debt); Section 3's human review gate, which is only honorable if the human has the condition to hold it. The relocation ledger lights Epistemic from the AI Development Gap. Closely tied to the Review-Capacity-as-condition stream (climate half: the owned situation model; weather half: finite attention).
 
#### Vocational Viability
**Question:** Does this work still feel like work I want to do?
 
**What's under threat:** Whether the work still pulls you, as it shifts from sustained problem-solving toward orchestration and review. The dimension most likely to be dismissed as soft — and, the page argues, the one most likely to predict who quietly leaves the field over the next decade.
 
**Signals:**
- What am I noting, week by week, about the texture of the work itself? — `Asked · Weekly`
- Does each session leave me energized, or depleted? — `Asked · Per Session`
- How much time am I spending on the craft outside paid hours? — `Auto · Weekly`
- Would I want to keep practicing exactly this way for another ten years? — `Asked · Annual`
**Special handling:** This dimension carries a **separate, granular consent** requirement; not all mentees will opt in, which is appropriate given the personal nature of the data. The reflection journal underlying Vocational tracking is the one stream on the cohort's *separate, optional consent track*.
 
**Corpus cross-reference:** The newest of the nine and the thinnest-seeded earlier — the relocation ledger's coverage note flags that no Section 2 situation points to it, making this dimension a harvest-forward axis rather than a situation-seeded one. Adjacent to the de-skilling / professional-identity material in the skill-acquisition stream. **This is the dimension most in need of a Clay-experience anchor** to carry it, since the corpus does not otherwise build toward it.
 
#### Identity Viability
**Question:** What is my value in this arrangement?
 
**What's under threat:** The sense that what you bring is still meaningful — that you can stand behind the work with pride when an agent writes the code and you steer. The page frames this less as "who am I over time" and more as "is what I bring still something I recognize as valuable, important, and mine."
 
**Signals:**
- When asked "what do you do?", how am I describing my contribution across public bios, headlines, and bylines? — `Auto · Monthly`
- How am I attributing ownership of my shipped work — "I built," "I orchestrated," "I shipped"? — `Auto · Monthly`
- Which role-frames am I using in agent transcripts — "as a developer," "as a product owner," "as a builder"? — `Auto · Monthly`
- What am I writing, annually, about the value of human contribution in AI-assisted practice? — `Asked · Annual`
**Corpus cross-reference:** Section 1's two jobs (origination and accountability — "am I still a builder if the machine does the producing?"). Section 2's Internal-Talent trade lights Identity alongside Cognitive in the relocation ledger.
 
### Family II — Viability of the Practice
 
*The family question: is what I sell still worth selling?*
 
#### Differential Viability
**Question:** When everyone can do this, what is the value of me doing it?
 
**What's under threat:** The portion of what you are paid that comes from being *early* rather than from being *skilled*. The early-adopter premium is, by definition, temporary.
 
**Signals:**
- How is my engagement pricing changing over time, normalized to comparable scope? — `Auto · Monthly`
- What kind of questions are prospects bringing me — capability-acquisition asks, or next-order questions? — `Auto · Monthly`
- How widely is my practice's distinctive vocabulary diffusing through public discourse? — `Auto · Monthly`
- What can I do today that I couldn't two years ago — and what fraction is still rare? — `Asked · Annual`
**Corpus cross-reference:** Section 2's External-Dev-Dependency situation — once the same agents are available to everyone, what distinguishes you from the next founder with the identical stack. The relocation ledger lights Differential (with Economic) from that situation.
 
#### Judgment Viability
**Question:** What happens when the models exercise the judgment I currently sell?
 
**What's under threat:** The architectural, strategic, and design judgment that AI tools currently accelerate but do not yet originate — and that is migrating into the models themselves at a rate nobody has fully reckoned with.
 
**Signals:**
- On the same problem, how does my work compare to the frontier model's — judged blindly by independent reviewers? — `Asked · Quarterly`
- On a locked set of judgment-heavy problems, how is the gap moving with each new frontier model release? — `Auto · Per Release`
- How often am I pushing back on the agent in ways that materially improve the planned code? — `Auto · Weekly`
- Each week, which three judgment calls did I make that the model wouldn't have? — `Asked · Weekly`
- Which categories of judgment — architectural, strategic, design, operational — are moving into the model first? — `Auto · Quarterly`
**Corpus cross-reference:** Section 2's Legacy-Software-Debt situation — modernizing with AI trades a system you couldn't see for one whose change decisions a model now makes on your behalf; how much of the judgment is still yours. The relocation ledger lights Judgment from that situation.
 
#### Temporal Viability
**Question:** Does the time I am supposedly saving actually accrue to me as time?
 
**What's under threat:** The promised dividend of AI-native practice — that productivity gains translate into recovered hours. Every prior productivity revolution suggests the dividend gets absorbed into rising expectation rather than reclaimed.
 
**Signals:**
- How are my hours distributed — reactive vs. generative, recovery vs. depletion? — `Auto · Weekly`
- Is my total number of work hours per week rising, falling, or holding? — `Auto · Weekly`
- How is my output-per-hour changing, on a defined output metric for the practice? — `Auto · Monthly`
- How many hours did I actually take back this quarter — and what did I do with them? — `Asked · Quarterly`
**Corpus cross-reference:** Section 2's SaaS-Vendor-Dependency situation — you trade a subscription for upkeep; the open question is whether the saved time actually accrues or gets eaten by the maintenance burden. The relocation ledger lights Temporal (with Economic) from that situation. Tied to the doctrine that *condition replaces time* as the binding constraint.
 
### Family III — Viability of the Conditions
 
*The family question: is the ground I'm standing on still ground?*
 
#### Economic Viability
**Question:** Will the conditions that make this profitable today continue to make it profitable tomorrow?
 
**What's under threat:** The unit economics of AI-native practice, currently subsidized by venture capital chasing market share. What happens when the subsidy ends and true cost meets list price.
 
**Signals:**
- How are my token costs per month moving when normalized against output? — `Auto · Monthly`
- What is my practice's gross margin, with AI tooling tracked as its own line item? — `Auto · Monthly`
- How are list prices on my dependencies changing month over month? — `Auto · Monthly`
- What signals — rate limits, deprecations, enterprise-tier introductions — are providers sending? — `Auto · Monthly`
**Corpus cross-reference:** The cost side of every Section 2 trade, most directly the SaaS and External-Dev situations. The relocation ledger lights Economic twice (from External Dev and SaaS Vendor) — the most-pointed-to dimension after Cognitive.
 
#### Ethical Viability
**Question:** Is what I'm doing defensible — to myself, to my clients, to the field, to the people downstream?
 
**What's under threat:** Whether the practice can answer for itself in every register it touches. The page names five, and notes the list keeps growing:
- *Environmental cost* — every token runs on a grid being expanded for AI demand, with utilities delaying coal retirements and breaking ground on new gas plants.
- *Labor displacement* — roles you would have paid humans for a year ago, you no longer pay anyone for; the dollars now go to a provider.
- *Provenance* — model outputs whose training inputs you cannot trace, attributed to you as if you made them.
- *Concentration of power* — decision-making pooling inside three or four labs whose internal debates set the rules.
- *Security* — code you did not write, shipped to systems that affect people you will never meet.
The page concedes the pushback already underway, especially among younger practitioners, is right — and that the practice does not get to declare itself ethical while the people downstream do not yet agree.
 
**Signals — self and disclosure:**
- What am I writing, annually, when I articulate my own ethical standing? — `Asked · Annual`
- How am I disclosing AI involvement in client deliverables? — `Auto · Monthly`
- How am I disclosing AI involvement in my public work — talks, posts, bios, bylines? — `Auto · Monthly`
- Of the output I ship, what fraction is AI-generated, AI-assisted, or fully human? — `Auto · Monthly`
- Where is the field shifting on attribution, labor displacement, and model-generated code in regulated systems? — `Auto · Monthly`
**Signals — environmental cost:**
- How many tokens am I generating per month, and what is the trend? — `Auto · Monthly`
- What is the estimated kWh and CO₂e of that month's compute, using each provider's published efficiency numbers? — `Auto · Monthly`
- When a smaller or more efficient model can do the job, am I choosing it? — `Auto · Monthly`
**Signals — labor, power, and consequence:**
- What roles or hours of work am I no longer paying humans for that I would have a year ago? — `Asked · Quarterly`
- How concentrated is my practice in any single provider's tooling, and do I have a working fallback if access were cut? — `Auto · Quarterly`
- What fraction of model-generated code from my practice am I personally reviewing before it reaches systems that affect people? — `Auto · Monthly`
**Corpus cross-reference:** Section 2's Prototype-to-Production-Wall situation — accountability in registers a prototype never owed (real user data, security, reliability, the consequences that land on people). The relocation ledger lights Ethical from that situation.

## 7. The two scales — subject and cohort
 
**Clay is the primary subject by default.** Every measurement runs against the same day-to-day corpus that produces the site's Resources — his work as technical co-founder of Soaring Titan and Maximum Automotive Intelligence.
 
**The cohort is optional and opt-in** (currently n=4). Mentees may opt in to extend the readings across the cohort, which makes the data richer. The governing rules:
- Consent is explicit and **granular** — by family and by signal, not all-or-nothing. The most personal readings (the Vocational reflection journal) sit on a separate, optional consent track.
- Mentee identity is preserved; mentee data never appears on the marketing surfaces of the site.
- Opting in is **never required** to be mentored — the engagement and the inquiry are decoupled by design.
- The consent instrument grants a right to withdraw at any time, with a choice between anonymize-in-place or full removal.
**Streams included when a mentee opts in:** agent transcripts, capability benchmarks, token-cost data, time-allocation data, and — on the separate optional track — the reflection journal underlying Vocational tracking.
 
This is the same instrument at two scales, built for the same reason: self-report lies in a population the same way it lies in one head (everyone reports they're fine), so the only honest reading of where this way of working takes people is the trailing one, taken across many practitioners over time.

## 8. The two surfaces — Research and Data
 
The inquiry publishes across two surfaces, captured in the page's line: *Research moves at the speed of thought; Data moves at the speed of evidence.* **Research names what is worth measuring; Data measures it.**
 
**Research — the discursive surface.** Original essays from practice and mentorship (with confidentiality preserved), curation of others' work, field signals, and eventually guest voices. Published *when ready, not on a schedule.* Every piece is tagged to one or more of the nine dimensions, so the feed can be filtered to whichever concern is live for the reader, across all three families. (The current archive entries and per-dimension counts on the staged page are placeholders illustrating the editorial plan.)
 
**Data — the empirical surface.** Quarterly trend lines tracking the same nine dimensions over time. Explicitly *not a dashboard, not branding* — and, critically, **not a sales surface**: the Data page carries no call-to-action, no schedule-a-call, no offer link, by design, because the measurement only stays honest if it isn't also selling something. Methodology is versioned (v1.0, Q1 2026) and built around per-dimension protocol documents.

## 9. The standing commitment (what makes it honest)
 
The pairing of Research and Data rests on a **pre-committed honesty statement**, posted in full on both surfaces and described on the Data page as the thing that makes the rest of the page worth reading — *"without it, what's above would be a vanity dashboard."* Its terms:
 
- **Unfavorable findings are published with the same visibility as favorable ones.**
- **Metrics are locked with a PhD collaborator before they go public** — so they can't be gamed after the fact.
- **Methodology changes are versioned and visible** — no silent revisions.
- The collaborator (a PhD in AI Ethics) **locks the metrics before publication and reviews every quarterly reading.**

## 10. How the model connects to the rest of the corpus
 
The nine dimensions are the **harvest** in the playbook's seed → bloom → harvest pedagogy. Nothing here is introduced cold:
 
- **The self of Section 1** is what's measured — the maintenance trilogy (condition, observability, upkeep) built the personal dashboard; the nine dimensions are that same dashboard read at career scale. The drunk-driver / self-report-lies principle is the spine of both.
- **The profile of Section 2** set the baseline — the Operating Profile's climate read is the zero-point each trend is scored against, and each situation's relocation trade named the dimension it would later light up (the relocation ledger books eight of the nine; Vocational is the deliberate exception).
- **The telemetry of Section 3** emits the signals — the ADLC board's quiet measurements (origination ratio, reframe-vs-accept rate, review-without-comment rate) are the daily version of the long-clock readings here. Review capacity sits as both an ADLC constraint and a condition signal, the through-line from Section 1 to this section.
- **The loop closes outward and inward.** Inward: a bad reading becomes the next thing worth originating, handing the long clock back to the short one — *Remaining* feeds *Becoming*. Outward: the reader's private instrument can extend Clay's public one, via the cohort, if they choose.
